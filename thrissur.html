Thrissur

Referred to as the cultural Capital of Kerala, famous for advanced traditional performing arts of Kerala, religious places and world-famous Thrissur Pooram and Onam festival, Thrissur is as beautiful as you could imagine or maybe more! Popular among pilgrims all over the world as three significant temples of Lord Shiva are located in Thrissur this one also is a holy city. Thrissur is an abbreviation of Thiru- Shiva- Per-Ur that signifies a city of three temples of God Shiva.

Thrissur is a place from where Kerala gets its charm of festivals. It was the capital of the Kingdom of Kochi, festivals here are celebrated in a very comprehensive manner. The celebrations of Thrissur are the major attractions for travellers, and your trip is incomplete if you will not witness the famous festivals of Thrissur. Tourist Attractions

Athirappilly Waterfalls

Located 60 km from Thrissur city, Athirapally Falls is a haven for flora and fauna alike. It is a popular picnic spot and the scenic beauty of the waterfall offers spectacular visuals for visitors.

Charpa Waterfalls

Charpa Falls is another one of the nature's delight that displays the blessings of Mother Nature. Cascading down from a shot height, this fall which is a part of River Chalakkudy offers a breathtaking experience. Athirappilly Falls and Lake Vachumaram are a stone's throw away from Charpa Falls.

Vadakkumnnathan Temple

With a collection of beautiful Mural paintings, some of which are more than 400 years old, this gigantic 1000 year old temple and historical structure is famous for the Nataraja Mural near the main gate.

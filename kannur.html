Kannur

Kannur is a beautiful and blissful destination in Kerala. It is blessed with unbonding beauty and charm that will cast a spell on you. From bays and beaches to museums and forts, it offers unique and interesting places to visit. Apart from that, there are a plethora of things to do in Kannur that you can indulge in to give yourself an escape from the daily routine. They will help you know about the culture of the town and make you desire to visit it again and again.

Kannur is also known as the ‘Manchester of Kerala’ as the handloom fabric produced here is the finest and is exported to many great countries. The best time to visit Kannur is from September to March as the weather would be pleasant during these months and you will be able to explore the destination comfortably. April to June are the summer months and would be humid whereas from June to August are monsoon months and the place experiences heavy rainfall.

Some of the best things to shop for in Kannur are Banana chips, tea, coffee, aroma oils, and Cashew nut. Kannur is famous around the world for its gold shops around the world so you can also shop for Kannur. It is also a place for food lovers, with delicious Thalassery Chicken Biryani, Unnakkaya, Malabar Beef roast, Kallumakkaya Nirachalthi, Chatti Pathiri, and Mutton Korma to savour.

Tourist Attractions

Payyambalam Beach

The impeccably unspoilt Payyabalam beach is adorned with sparkling pearls of golden sand and foamy white waves of the Arabian Sea. The highlight of Kannur and one of the many beautiful beaches of Kerala, it has an incredibly peaceful and inviting aura.

Muzhappilangad Beach

The Muzzhapilangad Drive-in Beach at Kannur is one referred to with many superlatives- as the longest drive-in beach in the whole of India, as one of the cleanest beaches of Kannur and most importantly, as one of the best drive-in beaches that the entire Asian continent has to offer.

Arakkal Museum

The Arakkal Museum in Kannur city is one of the most significant and frequently visited attractions of the south that celebrates the memory and grandeur of Kerala's only Muslim royal family- the Arakkal Ali Rajas. This museum is a testimony to the impeccable Muslim architecture and design.

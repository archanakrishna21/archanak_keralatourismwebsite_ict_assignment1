Idukki

A proud home of South Indiaâ€™s highest peak, Anamudi that stands at a height of 2,695 meters, Idukki is one of the most stunning hill stations in Kerala.Housing 13 other peaks with a height of over 2000 meters, it attracts tourist from across the globe for its wildlife sanctuaries, hill stations, spice plantation centers, and mountain treks.

Idukki is known for its scenic beauty and is believed to be a heaven for nature lovers. The climate of Idukki is Tropical. There is significant rainfall in most of the months of the year. The short dry season has little effect on the overall climate. The average annual temperature is 24.4 degrees Celsius in Idukki. The average of Precipitation here is 2226 mm.

Tourists from all over the world visit the place to spend time amidst its beautiful valleys, dense forests, and extensive wildlife. The place can be visited throughout the year except for the rainy season. However, the winter season is considered the best season to explore the beauty of Idukki. Famed for its panoramic beauty and everything green and pretty, Idukki is one of the most frequented districts among all the 14 districts in Kerala. A land of temples, dams, and waterfalls, the Idukki district in Kerala is a perfect spot to rejuvenate and replenish your soul at.

Tourist Attractions

Hill View Park

With spectacular views of the Cheruthoni and Idukki Dams, the Hill-view Park serves as a famous tourist destination of Idukki. One can also spot diverse wildlife in its belt natural habitat.

Kulamavu Dam

If you're a hiking or trekking junkie, Kulamavu is a hill-station that offers the perfect terrain for an adrenaline kick. With some amazing trekking trails, the place is also adorned with equally enchanting surroundings.

Iduki Arch Dam

Built across the Kuravan and Kurathi hills at a height of about 550 feet, the Idukki Arch Dam is an architectural genius, for the rugged terrain along which it is built. The natural beauty surrounding the dam is breath-taking.
